An initiative to make technology serve people, not the other way around.

Having spent a year traveling across 49 states to learn how technology is affecting local communities, I observed a consistent theme: organizations are drowning in software, overwhelmed by tech hype, and losing touch with the human element of their work.

I want to change that. I am offering my technical experience to help local nonprofits, small businesses, and community organizations solve real problems—with zero financial obligation.

If I can help you simplify or solve a problem, I will. If not, I'll point you toward someone who can.

No fees. No obligation. Just honest collaboration.

What We Can Work On

I don't believe in digital transformation for its own sake. Sometimes the best solution is a better workflow, a simpler tool, or removing technology entirely. I offer a grounded, honest board for:

  • Simplifying Messy Setups: Auditing your current software/hardware inventory to see what you can safely cut or consolidate.
  • Systems & Automation: Building clean, background workflows so your team can spend less time fighting spreadsheets and more time with people.
  • Websites & Strategy: Crafting practical digital spaces that serve your actual goals, not just aesthetic trends.
  • AI Tools & Literacy: Demystifying what AI actually can (and cannot) do for your organization, without the marketing hype.

Why Am I Doing This? (The "Catch")

I am an experienced software and DevOps engineer. I am financially stable and do not need this initiative to generate income.

This project is called SpiritualTech. It is built on the philosophy that technology should support human spirit, connection, and community quality of life—rather than forcing humans to serve an algorithm.

What I get out of this is simple:

  • Insight: Learning directly from real-world, localized operational challenges.
  • Relationships: Building genuine connections with people doing good work.
  • Impact: Leaving local organizations healthier and more self-sufficient than I found them.

Simple Expectations

To keep this collaboration honest and constructive, we operate on a few basic principles:

  • No Long-Term Dependency: I aim to empower your team or build systems that don't require my permanent oversight.
  • Selective Alignment: I accept projects based entirely on where I believe my skills can make a genuine, positive impact.
  • Absolute Confidentiality: Anything you share about your operations, data, or struggles remains completely private.
  • Total Ownership: Any ideas, roadmaps, or processes we co-create belong entirely to you. Take them and run with them.

How It Works

The Intro: Reach out or book a short conversation.

The Decision: If there is a clear, constructive way I can help, we map out a small, defined milestone and move forward.
