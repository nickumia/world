The Origin

This page is born out of my high school inviting me back for Career Day. There is a lot to be said about what I've gained throughout life so far, and I hope some of it may be useful for others on their own path.

The Two Paths of Learning

Throughout my journey, I've discovered that growth happens through two fundamental ways of learning. Understanding these paths has been invaluable in navigating my own career and life decisions; helping to create mental models for operating in different environments.

What It Looks Like

📖 Books (Captured Experience)

The accumulated wisdom and knowledge of generations, distilled into pages we can absorb at our own pace. These represent compressed experience across generations.

🎓 Education (Structured Knowledge)

Structured learning environments designed to impart foundational knowledge and critical thinking skills.

Stream of Knowledge → "Here's how I think the world works."

🤔 Decisions

The moments where you choose a direction; often without perfect information. Every decision becomes a step forward.

🎯 Actions & Results

Real understanding comes from acting on those decisions and experiencing the results, both successes and failures.

Awareness + Experience → "Here's how my world works."

Both paths converge within you. The true power lies in synthesizing external knowledge with internal experience.

Guiding this synthesis is mentorship: the art of helping others see what they cannot yet see, and navigate these learning journeys with greater wisdom.

The Foundation Years: My 20s

My twenties were defined by learning to make decisions and commit to them. It was a decade of exploration, building foundations, and understanding that perfect information rarely exists; sometimes you just have to choose and move forward.

⚖️

Learning to Decide

What It Looks Like

🎯 Analysis Paralysis

Early in my 20s, I struggled with endless research and overthinking. I learned that waiting for perfect certainty means waiting forever.

🚀 The 70% Rule

When you have 70% of the information you think you need, that's usually enough to make a good decision and adjust course.

🔄 Course Correction

Understanding that decisions aren't final; they're directional. The ability to pivot and adapt became my superpower.

The most crucial skill I developed wasn't making the "right" choice, but learning to make a choice and commit to it.

💼 Career Experiments

From software engineering to product management to entrepreneurship, each role taught me something valuable about myself and my capabilities.

🌍 Geographic Freedom

Living in different cities and working with diverse teams expanded my perspective beyond what any book could teach.

🧘 Personal Growth

Learning who I was outside of work, my values, my boundaries, and my non-negotiables, proved just as important as professional skills.

My 20s gave me permission to try, fail, and discover what truly mattered to me.

By the end of my 20s, I hadn't figured everything out; but I had built something far more valuable: a foundation of self-trust, adaptability, and clarity about what truly matters to me.

🎖️ Self-Trust

Confidence that I could handle whatever came my way

🔍 Clarity of Values

Understanding what I wouldn't compromise on

🤝 Relationship Skills

Learning to build meaningful connections and networks
