A Note
Hmm.. it would appear that life has plans for me that I'm discovering in real time.

Where shall I start? ...this isn't really about "what level of detail to share" as much as it's about "what's the best way to share the details given the experience I've gained". The more I travel, the more I understand that the answers are not out there. I've known this for a while and it doesn't make me want to travel less. But there's a different texture to life that I experience now.

It all started back in 2015. As with many of my adventures from the past, it started with a girl haha.. The details between 2015 and 2026 are not super important, since we also don't remember much as is 😅 There is just one important memory that I have from around the 2018 timeframe. It is very dreamlike. It may not even be a real memory; hence my forewarnings. There's an imagery that I have.

A desertlike landscape. A walkway loosely barracaded by those dividers that are on the highway. A faint festival ambience not too far off in the background. A tall warehouse factory-like building in a slightly different direction from the festival. The festival had these glowing lights that were warm and welcoming. But I (or whoever was in this memory) was not drawn to the festival. It was night and there was a chill in the air, but it wasn't cold.

I remember this scene being described to me and not one that I was really living myself. With that, there isn't a lot of talking or other activities that truly define the memory. Whether that is a real memory or not, it is a core memory of this entire experience and it is tied to the history leading to this experience.

Fast-forward to January 2026. I'm looking through emails leading up to Peru and I stumble across an old email from this girl from 2018. I reconnect (as I do almost habitually) and she responds as well. I mistake her for living in Mexico and she corrects me as she is actually in Guatemala. We start talking. It's very natural. The conversation flows so well. It comes up that we should meet up. The original plan was to meet somewhere internationally since we both enjoy traveling. However, with present life, we settled on me visiting her in Guatemala. And thus, this trip was born.

Vietnam wasn't on my radar. Peru was not really an active point on the radar. Guatemala was definitely not on my radar. Vietnam opened up SE Asia (Japan is almost a guarantee at this point. Spoiler: upcoming Hawaii trip will be the closest I get to SE Asia 🥺). Peru opened up New Zealand. Ecuador opened up more of South America. I had my eyes set hardcore on America and the world essentially opened while that happened. Here we are, almost one year after the US trip, and connections just keep opening up.

Coming to this trip though, we had big plans that got more realistic due to life events and the fact that we were meeting a stranger after so many years. It all worked out. The places, the people, the experiences. All of it turned out as natural as it could be. The joys. The spontaneity. The sorrows. More unexpected connections haha.. None of my blogs have really been a story narration of events, but there are some details worth capturing:

  • Guatemala gave off a very depressed vibe. Not as depressed as Mississippi. However, it was very very mellow, which may not be a bad thing. Just not my favorite vibe.
  • The stark difference between tourist-y Antigua and normal-life Guatemala City was also pretty shocking. Antigua was more chill. But Guatemala City was more real.
  • Hobbitenango was a bit disappointing. It felt like more of a journey to get up there than necessary; but maybe there's just an honest newness to the tourism that feels like it doesn't have its sea legs yet. And that's crazy given I was told that it was much more developed than 2 years ago.
  • I met couple from LA who were visiting for a Wedding. The guy was in HR, but gave off finance bro vibes, which is crazy cuz I was closer to the finance bro haha..
  • Guatemala City traffic is interesting. Everyone seems to always be using the same roads at the same times 🤷🏻‍♀️
  • I got to visit the Capitol building, which was a first for me internationally.
  • Guatemala was a bunch of active volcanos, but I wasn't able to visit any of them.
  • Things are both cheap and expensive. I didn't use as much money as I thought I would; but I also used more than I thought I would.
  • ...

There's something to be said about how personal travel is. I recently rewatched some of the videos from the road trip to prepare for a podcast recording tomorrow. And it was very interesting to see how much I had captured. How much of life I can relive because I did the work back then. If nothing else, vlogging to capture things for self-reflection is absolutely invaluable to me. This trip might not have meant much at the time. But it definitely will in the future. Just like the road trip, I didn't feel it's impact in realtime. Just like Vietnam, I complained about most of it while I was there. But carrying the experience and lessons learned means a lot to me.

I think a large part of it is not being able to understand what it means until there is context that gives it meaning. Also, there are cycles to gaining experience and this definitely felt like a middle trip. One that gives depth to the current cycle but doesn't define a new cycle.
