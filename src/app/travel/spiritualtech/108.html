Literally, there are not enough songs about Thanksgiving and this song was super cool when I found it. Thanksgiving has been an interesting theme over my life. From school, there's all of the history and theory that surrounds it that, for better or worse, doesn't get appreciated enough. From family, there have been a select few Thanksgivings that we celebrated with family that are memorable in their own ways. In recent years, I haven't done much to celebrate it, save for lots of reflection and thinking.

As I alluded to above, I have mixed memories about how the day has been; however, it is definitely meant to be a social holiday. I don't have any issues celebrating it solo (as I did on the trip), but it always makes me think about things, people and experiences. Even if a big family event doesn't happen, it's always nice to have people close and accessible.

In terms of thoughts for this day, it's funny.. I actually have two videos that I recorded that day that capture thoughts and it's very interesting the topics that I've focused on.

In general, during the trip, I didn't attempt to find specific connections between all of the things I was doing. However, I always new everything was connected. Reflecting now, it is wild to me how connected it is and how much I'm able to put pieces together. There's always the question of "free will" (i.e. how much am I "choosing" to think about the things that I am); however, that's a different conversation and, for what it's worth, free will in this context can probably best be described as the natural flow of thoughts and experiences. "Naturally" being that "I have chosen the things up til this point" which has made me "want to choose the things at this point."

Overall.. it was a fun day.. honestly I probably can say I enjoyed it more than Christmas (during the trip); but we'll come back to that point when I do that reflection :p
